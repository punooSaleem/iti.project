Industrial Training Institutes (ITIs) are government-recognized institutions that focus on providing practical and technical education to students. They are designed to equip learners with industry-relevant skills in trades such as electrician, fitter, welder, mechanic, and computer operator. Unlike traditional academic courses, ITIs emphasize hands-on training, enabling students to gain real-world experience and become job-ready in a shorter period of time. ITIs play a crucial role in strengthening the workforce of a country by producing skilled technicians who support various industries. These institutes follow a structured curriculum that combines theoretical knowledge with practical workshops, ensuring that students understand both the concepts and their application. Many ITIs also collaborate with industries to offer apprenticeship programs, which further enhance employability and bridge the gap between education and industry requirements.
